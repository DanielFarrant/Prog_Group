Financial Technology

Applying technology to improve financial activites.

Financial technology (“FinTech”) is an application of computers that aims to enhance the financial services industry for consumers, business owners and companies. Examples of key areas that have been improved by FinTech are:

  • insurance;
  • trading;
  • risk management; and
  • retail banking.

A Brief History of FinTech

At first glance FinTech appears to be an industry that has only been around for the last 20 years. However, technology has been changing the financial services industry from as early as the 1950s when credit cards were first made available to the public. This was followed by automatic teller machines (“ATMs”), electronic stock trading, and bank mainframe computers in the next few decades.

In more recent decades, the invention of the internet and the proliferation of smart devices has allowed for a wave of mobile banking apps from both traditional banks and new FinTech start-ups. These apps allow customers to do a range of tasks, e.g. checking their balance, transferring money etc, quickly and without having to visit their local bank branch. Additionally, FinTech start-ups are often competing with traditional banks to streamline financial services for the consumer. For example, Affirm offers immediate short-term loans for purchases to customers with poor or no credit. Thus, allowing people who would have difficulty acquiring a credit card a viable alternative.

The Future

The future for the FinTech industry looks bright; global FinTech funding reached a new high in the first quarter of 2018. However, it is important to note that traditional banking will not be disappearing anytime soon, though it has and will continue to be disrupted by FinTech start-ups. Areas of innovation in FinTech include:

  • cryptocurrency;
  • RegTech;
  • InsurTech;
  • robo-advisors; and
  • blockchain technology.
