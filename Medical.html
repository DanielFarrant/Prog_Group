Medical Technology

Applications of technology in medical sciences.

Medical technology is any piece of technology which can aid the lives of people suffering from from a range of ailments. This can include psychological and physical conditions, and is currently being used (or developed) to either diagnose, monitor or treat almost every medical issue that affects us. Medical technology can encompass things such as latex gloves, plasters, even scalpels, but I will primarily focus on the more high-tech applications of medical technology which tie more into computer science.

Overview of medical technology

The applications of medical technology are extremely prevalent, there are very few people in the modern world who haven't at some point been influenced by it's impact. These range from things such as x-rays, to pacemakers and replacement limbs. There are a large variety of applications which are made with the purpose of extending and improving the quality of people's lives. Things such as diagnosis of diseases through analysing blood samples are also things only possible due to the presence of medical technology.

The Future

In the future we can only expect the applications of medical technology to become more diverse, by the end of our lifetimes there will be extremely significant improvements on currently existing technology and new technology to tackle old and new health problems. In the past 100 or so years blood types were discovered, penicillin was discovered, the first liver transplant occurred. People in the future will look back on the technology we use now and be surprised at how little we were able to do.

Some of the ideas that we may eventually see used in the future are:

  • Solutions to aging
  • Ways to alter the human genome
  • Home scanners which can be read by doctors remotely
  • Growing organs from stem cells
  • Advancements in exoskeletons for patients with paralysis
